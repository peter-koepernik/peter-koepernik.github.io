I'm a final year PhD student in Mathematics at Oxford University specialised in probability theory, and I'm currently visiting the Center for Human-Compatible AI at Berkeley AI Research to work on reinforcement learning and tree search algorithms with Cameron Allen and Stuart Russel.

Before I started getting interested in AI research, I worked on mathematical population biology with Alison Etheridge, and on numerical stochastic analysis with Terry Lyons.

Prior to starting my PhD, I obtained a Master's in Mathematics from Oxford University, where I finished at rank 1 of my cohort. Even further in the past, I obtained B.Sc. degrees in Mathematics, Computer Science, and Physics (yes, three separate, full-time degrees; do not recommend if you enjoy free time) from the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology.
